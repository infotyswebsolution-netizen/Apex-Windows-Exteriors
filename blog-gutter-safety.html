How Clogged Gutters Destroy Your Home's Foundation

By Apex Expert Team | February 28, 2026

Most Ontario homeowners view gutter cleaning as a tedious chore. However, eavestroughs (gutters) are actually your home's first line of defense against the #1 cause of residential property damage: Water.

The Foundation Connection

The primary purpose of gutters is to collect rainwater from your roof and direct it away from the base of your house. When gutters are clogged with leaves, silt, and debris, water overflows. Instead of being piped away, it pours directly down the side of your house, saturating the ground around your foundation.

Over time, this saturated soil exerts hydrostatic pressure on your basement walls. This leads to:

  • Horizontal cracks in the foundation
  • Basement leaks and flooding
  • Mold and mildew growth in the lower levels
  • Soil erosion that can cause your house to shift or settle unevenly

Wood Rot and Siding Damage

Overflowing water doesn't just hit the ground. It splashes back against your siding and fascia boards. Constant moisture leads to wood rot, paint peeling, and can even attract wood-eating insects like carpenter ants.

The Solution: Gutter Guards

While manual cleaning twice a year is better than nothing, the permanent solution is high-quality micro-mesh gutter guards. These allow water to flow freely while blocking even the smallest debris like pine needles and maple keys.
