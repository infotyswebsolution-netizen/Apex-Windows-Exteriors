Windows for KW's Diverse Housing Stock

Downtown Kitchener Century Homes

The older residential streets near downtown Kitchener — Victoria Park, Fairview, Vanier — feature original 1920s–1950s brick homes with wood-frame windows that are decades overdue for replacement. Many still have single-pane glass or early aluminum frames that provide almost no thermal resistance. Brick-to-brick window replacement in these homes requires careful sizing to match existing rough openings while meeting modern energy code requirements.

Waterloo New Builds

Newer Waterloo subdivisions use standard vinyl windows — but the entry-level builder-grade windows installed at construction are often the minimum specification. Upgrading to premium frames with better seals and triple-pane glass in north Waterloo (where winter exposures are more pronounced) yields measurable annual energy savings.

Vinyl Siding in Kitchener-Waterloo

The KW market has seen a dramatic increase in vinyl siding upgrades as homeowners replace aging aluminum cladding on 1970s–1990s-era homes. Modern insulated vinyl siding combines a wood-grain or smooth aesthetic with embedded polystyrene foam — adding R-3 to R-5 to wall assemblies that were often originally built with no exterior insulation whatsoever.

We complete full-home vinyl siding installations near Kitchener and Waterloo including soffit, fascia, and eavestrough integration for a complete watertight roofline system.
