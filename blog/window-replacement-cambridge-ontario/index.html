What Window Types Work Best for Cambridge Homes?

Cambridge's architectural mix — from century-era brick homes in Old Galt to newer builds in Hespeler — demands flexibility in window profiles. Here is what we recommend by home type:

Double-Hung Windows

The most popular choice in Cambridge's older brick homes. Both sashes open for ventilation, enabling easy cleaning from inside, and modern double-hung vinyl windows achieve exceptional air-sealing performance far superior to the aluminum originals from the 1960s–1980s.

Casement Windows

Ideal for modern Cambridge builds and finished basements. Crank-style side hinges create an airtight seal when closed — eliminating the small but significant air gap that double-hung windows always carry. Casement windows are the best-performing option for energy efficiency per dollar.

Triple-Pane Windows

Given Cambridge's position in the inland cold zone of Southern Ontario, triple-pane glass is increasingly the recommended upgrade. The additional glass layer provides measurably superior R-values (R-7 vs R-3.5 for standard double-pane), cutting heating bills by up to 25% in older Cambridge homes.

4 Signs Your Cambridge Home Needs Windows Now

  • Condensation between panes: Indicates sealed unit failure — the argon gas has escaped. The glass is now providing almost zero insulation value.
  • Difficult operation: If windows stick, won't lock, or won't stay open, the frame has distorted from moisture infiltration.
  • Visible drafts in winter: Hold your hand near the frame edge during a cold day. If you feel cold air moving, your weather stripping has completely failed.
  • Rot or soft wood: Interior wood frames that feel spongy or show paint bubbling are actively absorbing moisture — every additional winter accelerates the damage.
