Steel vs Fiberglass Doors: What's Best for Ontario Winters?

Expert Insights • Apex Windows & Exteriors

Making the Right Choice for Your Front Entryway

Choosing a new front door is one of the highest-impact exterior decisions you can make. When installing an exterior door in Ontario, your choice fundamentally comes down to two Titans: Steel or Fiberglass.

1. The Case for Steel Doors

Steel doors are the ultimate guardians. They provide unparalleled structural security and are generally the most cost-effective option on the market. They are injected with dense polyurethane foam, making them inherently robust against the frigid temperatures of the Golden Horseshoe.

The drawback: They can dent under severe direct impacts, and if scratched down to the bare metal, they are susceptible to rust (though modern factory priming heavily mitigates this).

2. The Case for Fiberglass Doors

Fiberglass is considered the premium tier. It is absolutely impervious to rust, resists large dents, and will never expand or contract during sweeping temperature swings. A massive aesthetic advantage is that fiberglass can be richly stained to perfectly emulate an authentic mahogany or oak wood-grain finish.

The Final Verdict

If you're upgrading on a strict budget or heavily prioritize modern smooth finishes, steel is fantastic. If you want a traditional architectural look with zero maintenance requirements over the next 20 years, fiberglass represents a permanent upgrade.
