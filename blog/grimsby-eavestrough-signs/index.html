3 Signs Your Grimsby Home Needs New Eavestroughs Immediately

Expert Insights • Apex Windows & Exteriors

Protecting Your Foundation from Water Damage

Your eavestrough system is the literal lifeline protecting your roof, siding, and foundation from exponential water damage. Because Grimsby experiences localized storms and a heavy tree canopy sitting near the escarpment, failing gutters deteriorate homes rapidly.

1. Visible Sagging and Pulling Away

If you can visibly see a physical gap appearing between your fascia board and the eavestrough channel, the structural integrity of the spike or hangar system has failed completely. This is usually caused by excessive weight from ice buildup or heavily saturated leaves during fall.

2. Waterfall Spillage During Rain

If water cascades straight over the side of the gutters during a standard downpour, the downspouts are entirely choked. Over time, that water directly bashes the soil near your basement foundation, eroding grading and triggering basement leaks. Regular eavestrough cleaning is the primary preventative mechanism.

3. Peeling Paint Underneath the Roofline

Water constantly tracking behind the gutter system will slowly strip exterior paint. If the wood along your soffit or fascia feels soft, looks stained, or shows peeling paint, you have a chronic moisture trap occurring behind the aluminum.

The Solution

If these signs are prominent, consider upgrading entirely to a 5-inch seamless eavestrough system heavily integrated with premium gutter guards to ensure zero failure points.
